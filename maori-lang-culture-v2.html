Strategic & Legal Context

The B23 baseline of $165M reflects Labour's Maihi Karauna strategy — the Crown's Māori language strategy committing to a goal of one million te reo speakers by 2040. The coalition has not formally repudiated this strategy, but has defunded the machinery required to achieve it. The bilingual towns programme was the most ambitious attempt to create immersive language environments outside the home and classroom; its 64% cut, alongside the removal of te reo signage funding and reductions to broadcasting, removes the transmission infrastructure that living language revitalisation depends on.

For CERD and UNDRIP purposes: ICERD Article 5 protects equality in the enjoyment of cultural rights; UNDRIP Articles 13–15 establish Indigenous peoples' rights to revitalise, use, develop, and transmit their languages and cultures. The pattern here — nominal Crown strategy intact, funding infrastructure removed — is precisely the kind of de facto breach that shadow reporting is designed to surface. The Crown cannot claim compliance with language rights obligations while systematically defunding the conditions under which language transmission is possible.
